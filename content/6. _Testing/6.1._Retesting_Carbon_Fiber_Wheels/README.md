On second look at previous data, the carbon fiber wheels deflected more than the aluminium rims. This contradicts general consensus that carbon fiber is a stiffer material. When comparing simulations, the carbon wheels do deflect more but not to the degree of the experimental values. The experiment was rerun to ensure proper data collection.

Based on the second experiment, the carbon rims still deflected twice as much as the aluminium rims. On closer analysis, this is due to the concentricity of the wheels. The applicator rests nicely on the alu wheels but a small gap can be seen on the carbon fiber wheels. Although CNC machining is the most accurate machining method for post processing, it is not perfect and causes a small misalignment between the inner and outer barrels. At the most extreme, it can cause an additional deflection of xxmm before both of the applicator faces are in contact with the carbon fiber wheel.
