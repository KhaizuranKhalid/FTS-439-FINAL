## **Formula SAE**

FSAE is an intervarsity competition organised by SAE International, where teams design, manufacture,
build and race formula-style race cars. Each car will be judged on its design, cost, efficiency,
and performance through a series of dynamic and static events, as well as its compliance during
technical inspection. Team members will also be judged on their capabilities in team management,
design thinking, technical knowledge and engineering practices. 
<div></div>

The competition is evaluated based on the following scoring systems:

**Static Events**
| Event        | Description                                                                              | Points |
|--------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Presentation | Deliver a business, logistical, production or technical case that will attract outsiders | 75     |
| Cost         | Consider budget and incorporate production considerations for production and efficiency  | 100    |
| Design       | Illustrate use of engineering to meet design goals and understanding of vehicle design   | 150    |

<p className="italic">Table 1: Static Scoring System.</p>
</div>

**Dynamic Events**
| Event        | Description                                                                 | Points |
|--------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Acceleration | 75m course to evaluate vehicle straight line acceleration                    | 100    |
| Skid Pad     | Measure vehicle cornering ability on a flat surface with constant radius turn    | 75     |
| Autocross    | Evaluate vehicle maneuverability and handling qualities on a tight course   | 125    |
| Endurance    | Evaluate overall performance of vehicle and test durability and reliability | 275    |
| Efficiency   | Evaluate energy used to complete Endurance event                            | 100    |

## **NUS FSAE**

NUS Formula SAE is an engineering project under the Engineering Design and Innovation Centre
(EDIC) comprising a multi-disciplinary team of Electrical, Mechanical and Engineering Science students. The R25e team has a total of 27 team members with 13 fourth year students and 14 third year students.

## **Future Systems (FS) Department**

The Future Systems Department is a new department aimed at researching, developing and testing new and emerging
technologies and methods. This reduces the strain on other departments, allowing them to focus on designing the
upcoming car. Since its inception, FS has been working on CF rims.
