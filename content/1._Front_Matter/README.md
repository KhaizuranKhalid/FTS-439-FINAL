## **Abstract**

This thesis documents the design and optimisation of Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer rims for the R26e Race Car. The objective
is to reduce mass in a cost-effective manner through the use of dry carbon fiber fabric with the VARTM process. The current work involves creating a material library to have material data for
simulations. Future work will use that data to optimise the plies within the rim to create a rim that it over 10% lighter than
the current aluminium rims.
