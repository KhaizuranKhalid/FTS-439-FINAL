Although Concept 1 will create the strongest and lightest possible product with the best possible finish, it ultimately
does not meet our goal of cost effectiveness. This is due to the cost of the prepreg as previously mentioned as well as
the cost of creating a new mould. Additionally, a large factor that prevents the implementation of this
concept is modularity. The 3-piece design allows for the tire to the rim without the need of a mechanic. With a 1-piece design, specialised tools are required to mount the tire. Additionally, there are no workhops in Singapore with experience in mounting tires on carbon fiber wheels. For fear of damaging the carbon fiber rims, we ultimately decided with Concept 2.

Concept 2 is chosen for its balance of strength, cost and modularity. The 3-piece design will allow the tires to be mounted in-house, reducing operational costs. The use of dry carbon fiber fabric in the VARTM process can produce a similarly strong part at a lower cost than prepreg carbon fiber. As the moulds will be reused, this reduces the initial cost of the project. Finally, in the event of a crash, it would 
be possible to replace the damaged components rather than the whole rim. All of these factors contribute to the
department goal of cost-effectiveness.
