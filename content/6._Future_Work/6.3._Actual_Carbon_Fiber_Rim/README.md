After the layups are confirmed, the manufacturing of the carbon fiber rims can begin.
The VARTM process is similar to the prepreg process but requires a few tweaks. Since
the dry fabric does not have epoxy, an epoxy spray is needed to tack the fabric
to the mould surface. This will be repeated up to a thickness of 7 layers which is good
practice to ensure proper infusion of the epoxy into the plies. The first 7 layers of alternating 0deg and 45deg directions
will be cured
before carrying on with the remaining layers. The cured part will be sent of to be machined
into the final product. The 0/45 orientation creates a more even Young's modulus as the carbon fiber is only strong along its fiber directions.
